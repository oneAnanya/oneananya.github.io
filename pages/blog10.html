Sorting a 3x3 Array

Assume you have a 3x3 grid of numbers, like a small Sudoku puzzle. Numbers are in cells and your task is to rearrange these numbers so they go from the smallest to the largest through rows.

We can solve this using an easy way:

1. Initially, all the numbers that are on the grid will be written in one line just like when we list out all the numbers.

2. Following is already, we now need to put them into an ascending order.

3. Lastly, fill the sorted numbers into the 3x3 grids again by row filling.

To illustrate, if you had many cards with different digits on them and shuffled them to arrange them in sequence before then putting them back neatly across rows. So it’s like having several number cards which were jumbled until they were ordered numerically and then placed in straight lines once more, aren’t they?

This method will give us our initial number back but arranged in a proper manner hence making it easier for us to work with or understand. It feels like cleaning up your room or arranging your toys so that finding them becomes quite easy!
